visible spectrum is band electromagnetic spectrum that
is visible to human eye electromagnetic radiation this range
wavelengths is called visible light or simply light optical
spectrum is sometimes considered to be same as visible
spectrum but some authors define term more broadly to include
ultraviolet and infrared parts electromagnetic spectrum as well
known collectively as optical radiation
a typical human eye will respond to wavelengths from about to about
nanometers terms frequency this corresponds to a band
vicinity terahertz these boundaries are not sharply
defined and may vary per individual under optimal conditions
these limits human perception can extend to nm ultraviolet and
nm near infrared
spectrum does not contain all colors that human visual
system can distinguish unsaturated colors such as pink or purple
variations like magenta for example are absent because they can only
be made from a mix multiple wavelengths colors containing only one
wavelength are also called pure colors or spectral colors
visible wavelengths pass largely unattenuated through earth s
atmosphere via optical window region electromagnetic
spectrum an example this phenomenon is when clean air scatters blue
light more than red light and so midday sky appears blue apart
from area around sun which appears white because light is
not scattered as much optical window is also referred to as
visible window because it overlaps human visible response
spectrum near infrared nir window lies just out human
vision as well as medium wavelength infrared mwir window and
long wavelength or far infrared lwir or fir window although
other animals may perceive them

history
px newton s color circle png newton s color circle from opticks
showing colors he associated with musical notes
spectral colors from red to violet are divided by notes
musical scale starting at d circle completes a full octave from
d to d newton s circle places red at one end spectrum next to
violet at other this reflects fact that non spectral purple
colors are observed when red and violet light are mixed
th century roger bacon theorized that rainbows were produced
by a similar process to passage light through glass or
crystal
th century isaac newton discovered that prisms could
disassemble and reassemble white light and described phenomenon
his book opticks he was first to use word spectrum latin for
appearance or apparition this sense print
describing his experiments optics newton observed that when a
narrow beam sunlight strikes face a glass prism at an angle
some is reflected and some beam passes into and through
glass emerging as different colored bands newton hypothesized light
to be made up corpuscles particles different colors with
different colors light moving at different speeds transparent
matter red light moving more quickly than violet glass result
is that red light is bent refracted less sharply than violet as it
passes through prism creating a spectrum colors
px newton prismatic colours jpg newton s observation prismatic
colors david brewster
newton originally divided spectrum into six named colors red
orange yellow green blue and violet he later added indigo as
seventh color since he believed that seven was a perfect number as
derived from ancient greek sophists there being a connection
between colors musical notes known objects solar
system and days week human eye is relatively
insensitive to indigo s frequencies and some people who have
otherwise good vision cannot distinguish indigo from blue and violet
for this reason some later commentators including isaac asimov
have suggested that indigo should not be regarded as a color its own
right but merely as a shade blue or violet evidence indicates that
what newton meant by indigo and blue does not correspond to
modern meanings those color words comparing newton s observation
prismatic colors with a color image visible light spectrum shows
that indigo corresponds to what is today called blue whereas his
blue corresponds to cyan
th century johann wolfgang von goethe wrote about optical
spectra his theory colours goethe used word spectrum
spektrum to designate a ghostly optical afterimage as did
schopenhauer on vision and colors goethe argued that continuous
spectrum was a compound phenomenon where newton narrowed beam
light to isolate phenomenon goethe observed that a wider aperture
produces not a spectrum but rather reddish yellow and blue cyan edges
with white between them spectrum appears only when these edges are
close enough to overlap
early th century concept visible spectrum became
more definite as light outside visible range was discovered and
characterized by william herschel infrared and johann wilhelm ritter
ultraviolet thomas young thomas johann seebeck and others
young was first to measure wavelengths different colors

visible spectrum is limited to wavelengths that can both reach
retina and trigger visual phototransduction excite a visual opsin
insensitivity to uv light is generally limited by transmission through
lens insensitivity to ir light is limited by spectral
sensitivity functions visual opsins range is defined
psychometrically by luminous efficiency function which accounts
for all these factors humans there is a separate function for
each two visual systems one for photopic vision used daylight
which is mediated by cone cells and one for scotopic vision used
dim light which is mediated by rod cells each these functions have
different visible ranges however discussion on visible range
generally assumes photopic vision

each opsin has a spectral sensitivity function which defines how
likely it is to absorb a photon each wavelength luminous
efficiency function is approximately superposition
contributing visual opsins variance position individual
opsin spectral sensitivity functions therefore affects luminous
efficiency function and visible range for example long wave
red limit changes proportionally to position l opsin
positions are defined by peak wavelength wavelength highest
sensitivity so as l opsin peak wavelength blue shifts by nm
long wave limit visible spectrum also shifts nm large
deviations l opsin peak wavelength lead to a form color
blindness called protanomaly and a missing l opsin protanopia
shortens visible spectrum by about nm at long wave limit

different definitions
regardless actual physical and biological variance definition
limits is not standard and will change depending on
industry for example some industries may be concerned with practical
limits so would conservatively report nm while
others may be concerned with psychometrics and achieving broadest
spectrum would liberally report or even nm
luminous efficiency function nir does not have a hard
cutoff but rather an exponential decay such that function s value
or vision sensitivity at nm is about times weaker than at
nm much higher intensity is therefore required to perceive
nm light than nm light
vision outside visible spectrum
under ideal laboratory conditions subjects may perceive infrared light
up to at least nm while nm nir light can evoke red
suggesting direct absorption by l opsin there are also reports
that pulsed nir lasers can evoke green which suggests two photon
absorption may be enabling extended nir sensitivity
similarly young subjects may perceive ultraviolet wavelengths down to
about nm but detection light below nm
may be due to fluorescence ocular media rather than direct
absorption uv light by opsins as uva light is absorbed by
ocular media lens and cornea it may fluoresce and be released at a
lower energy longer wavelength that can then be absorbed by
opsins for example when lens absorbs nm light
fluorescence emission spectrum is centered on nm
non visual light detection
addition to photopic and scotopic systems humans have other
systems for detecting light that do not contribute to primary
visual system for example melanopsin has an absorption range
nm and regulates circadian rhythm and other reflexive
processes since melanopsin system does not form images it is
not strictly considered vision and does not contribute to visible
range

spectrum than humans respectively

mammals
most mammals have retained only two opsin classes lws and vs due
likely to nocturnal bottleneck however old world primates
including humans have since evolved two versions lws to
regain trichromacy unlike most mammals rodents uvs opsins have
remained at shorter wavelengths along with their lack uv filters
lens mice have a uvs opsin that can detect down to nm while
allowing uv light to reach retina can lead to retinal damage
short lifespan mice compared with other mammals may minimize this
disadvantage relative to advantage uv vision dogs have two
cone opsins at nm and nm so see almost entire visible
spectrum humans despite being dichromatic horses have two
cone opsins at nm and nm yielding a slightly more truncated
red vision

invertebrates
visual systems invertebrates deviate greatly from vertebrates
so direct comparisons are difficult however uv sensitivity has been
reported most insect species bees and many other insects can
detect ultraviolet light which helps them find nectar flowers
plant species that depend on insect pollination may owe reproductive
success to their appearance ultraviolet light rather than how
colorful they appear to humans bees long wave limit is at about
nm mantis shrimp exhibit up to opsins enabling a visible
range less than nm to above nm

main article spectroscopy
spectroscopy is study objects based on spectrum color
they emit absorb or reflect visible light spectroscopy is an
important tool astronomy as is spectroscopy at other wavelengths
where scientists use it to analyze properties distant objects
chemical elements and small molecules can be detected astronomical
objects by observing emission lines and absorption lines for example
helium was first detected by analysis spectrum sun
shift frequency spectral lines is used to measure doppler
shift redshift or blueshift distant objects to determine their
velocities towards or away from observer astronomical spectroscopy
uses high dispersion diffraction gratings to observe spectra at very
high spectral resolutions
