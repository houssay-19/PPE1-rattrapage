main article solar constant

incoming solar electromagnetic radiation per unit area that would be
incident on a plane perpendicular to rays at a distance one

just visible light its average value was thought to be
approximately w m varying slightly with solar activity
but recent recalibrations relevant satellite observations
indicate a value closer to w m is more realistic

life on earth
px d c bclmen c g c b versheide jpg sunlight
penetrating through a forest canopy germany
existence nearly all life on earth is fueled by light from
sun most autotrophs such as plants use energy sunlight
combined with carbon dioxide and water to produce simple sugars a
process known as photosynthesis these sugars are then used as
building blocks and other synthetic pathways that allow organism
to grow
heterotrophs such as animals use light from sun indirectly by
consuming products autotrophs either by consuming autotrophs
by consuming their products or by consuming other heterotrophs
sugars and other molecular components produced by autotrophs are
then broken down releasing stored solar energy and giving
heterotroph energy required for survival this process is known as
cellular respiration
prehistory humans began to further extend this process by putting
plant and animal materials to other uses they used animal skins for
warmth for example or wooden weapons to hunt these skills allowed
humans to harvest more sunlight than was possible through
glycolysis alone and human population began to grow
during neolithic revolution domestication plants and
animals further increased human access to solar energy fields devoted
to crops were enriched by inedible plant matter providing sugars and
nutrients for future harvests animals that had previously provided
humans with only meat and tools once they were killed were now used for
labour throughout their lives fueled by grasses inedible to humans
fossil fuels are remnants ancient plant and animal matter
formed using energy from sunlight and then trapped within earth for
millions years

effects on human health
main article health effects sunlight exposure
ultraviolet radiation sunlight has both positive and negative
health effects as it is both a principal source vitamin d and a
mutagen a dietary supplement can supply vitamin d without this
mutagenic effect but bypasses natural mechanisms that would
prevent overdoses vitamin d generated internally from sunlight
vitamin d has a wide range positive health effects which include
strengthening bones and possibly inhibiting growth some
cancers sun exposure has also been associated with
timing melatonin synthesis maintenance normal circadian rhythms
and reduced risk seasonal affective disorder
insufficient sun exposure is responsible for deaths
united states and deaths europe per year additionally a
lack sun has an increases risk for breast cancer colorectal cancer
hypertension cardiovascular disease metabolic syndrome multiple
sclerosis alzheimer s disease autism asthma type diabetes and
myopia
long term sunlight exposure is known to be associated with
development skin cancer skin aging immune suppression and eye
diseases such as cataracts and macular degeneration short term
overexposure is cause sunburn snow blindness and solar
retinopathy citation needed
uv rays and therefore sunlight and sunlamps are only listed
carcinogens that are known to have health benefits and a number
public health organizations state that there needs to be a balance
between risks having too much sunlight or too little there
is a general consensus that sunburn should always be avoided
epidemiological shows that people who have more exposure to
sunlight have less high blood pressure and cardiovascular related
mortality while sunlight and its uv rays are a risk factor for skin
cancer sun avoidance may carry more a cost than benefit for
over all good health a study found that there is no evidence
that uv reduces lifespan contrast to other risk factors like
smoking alcohol and high blood pressure
