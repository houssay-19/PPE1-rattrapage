refraction involves two related parts both a result wave nature

light slows as it travels through a medium other than vacuum such
as air glass or water this is not because scattering or
absorption rather it is because as an electromagnetic
oscillation light itself causes other electrically charged
particles such as electrons to oscillate oscillating
electrons emit their own electromagnetic waves which interact with
original light resulting combined wave has a lower speed
when light returns to a vacuum and there are no electrons nearby
this slowing effect ends and its speed returns to c
when light enters a slower medium at an angle one side
wavefront is slowed before other this asymmetrical slowing
light causes it to change angle its travel once light
is within new medium with constant properties it travels a
straight line again

refractive index air depends on air density and thus vary
with air temperature and pressure since pressure is lower at
higher altitudes refractive index is also lower causing light
rays to refract towards earth surface when traveling long distances
through atmosphere this shifts apparent positions stars
slightly when they are close to horizon and makes sun visible
before it geometrically rises above horizon during a sunrise

over a fire engine exhaust or when opening a window on a cold day
this makes objects viewed through mixed air appear to shimmer or
move around randomly as hot and cold air moves this effect is also
visible from normal variations air temperature during a sunny day
when using high magnification telephoto lenses and is often limiting
image quality these cases a similar way atmospheric
