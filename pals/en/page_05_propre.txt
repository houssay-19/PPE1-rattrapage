optics is branch physics that studies behaviour
manipulation and detection electromagnetic radiation including its
interactions with matter and instruments that use or detect it
optics usually describes behaviour visible ultraviolet and
infrared light study optics extends to other forms
electromagnetic radiation including radio waves
microwaves and x rays term optics is also applied to
technology for manipulating beams elementary charged particles
most optical phenomena can be accounted for by using classical
electromagnetic description light however complete electromagnetic
descriptions light are often difficult to apply practice
practical optics is usually done using simplified models most
common these geometric optics treats light as a collection rays
that travel straight lines and bend when they pass through or
reflect from surfaces physical optics is a more comprehensive model
light which includes wave effects such as diffraction and interference
that cannot be accounted for geometric optics historically
ray based model light was developed first followed by wave
model light progress electromagnetic theory th century
led to discovery that light waves were fact electromagnetic
radiation
some phenomena depend on light having both wave like and particle like
properties explanation these effects requires quantum mechanics
when considering light s particle like properties light is
modelled as a collection particles called photons quantum optics
deals with quantum mechanics to optical systems
optical science is relevant to and studied many related disciplines
including astronomy various engineering fields photography and
medicine especially radiographic methods such as beam radiation
therapy and ct scans and physiological optical fields
ophthalmology and optometry practical applications optics are found
a variety technologies and everyday objects including mirrors
lenses telescopes microscopes lasers and fibre optics
