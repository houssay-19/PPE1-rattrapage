main article diffuse reflection
when light strikes surface a non metallic material it bounces
off all directions due to multiple reflections by microscopic
irregularities inside material e g grain boundaries a
polycrystalline material or cell or fiber boundaries an organic
material and by its surface if it is rough thus an image is not
formed this is called diffuse reflection exact form
reflection depends on structure material one common model
for diffuse reflection is lambertian reflectance which light is
reflected with equal luminance photometry or radiance
radiometry all directions as defined by lambert s cosine law
light sent to our eyes by most objects we see is due to
diffuse reflection from their surface so that this is our primary
mechanism physical observation

main article retroreflector
some surfaces exhibit retroreflection structure these surfaces
is such that light is returned direction from which it came
when flying over clouds illuminated by sunlight region seen around
aircraft s shadow will appear brighter and a similar effect may be
seen from dew on grass this partial retro reflection is created by
refractive properties curved droplet s surface and reflective
properties at backside droplet
some animals retinas act as retroreflectors see tapetum lucidum for
more detail as this effectively improves animals night vision
since lenses their eyes modify reciprocally paths
incoming and outgoing light effect is that eyes act as a strong
retroreflector sometimes seen at night when walking wildlands with
a flashlight
a simple retroreflector can be made by placing three ordinary mirrors
mutually perpendicular to one another a corner reflector image
produced is inverse one produced by a single mirror a surface
can be made partially retroreflective by depositing a layer tiny
refractive spheres on it or by creating small pyramid like structures
both cases internal reflection causes light to be reflected back
to where it originated this is used to make traffic signs and
automobile license plates reflect light mostly back direction
from which it came this perfect retroreflection is not
desired since light would then be directed back into
headlights an oncoming car rather than to driver s eyes

complex conjugate reflection
this process which is also known as phase conjugation light
bounces exactly back direction from which it came due to a
nonlinear optical process not only direction light is
reversed but actual wavefronts are reversed as well a conjugate
reflector can be used to remove aberrations from a beam by reflecting
it and then passing reflection through aberrating optics a
second time if one were to look into a complex conjugating mirror it
would be black because only photons which left pupil would
reach pupil
